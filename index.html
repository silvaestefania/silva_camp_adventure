The Adventure Camp

A full day of exploring, building & making memories!

9:00
AM
Camp Check-In & DIY Gear
Both Kids
🧻 The Craft

Every camper needs gear! Tape two toilet paper rolls together and let them decorate their very own binoculars.

🖊️ 9-Year-Old

Markers, stickers & ties on a yarn lanyard for their binoculars.

🟡 2-Year-Old

Big stickers and dot markers — the more colorful, the better!

🌿 Independent Play

Once made, they explore the yard through their binoculars while you prep the next station.

9:40
AM
Sensory Bin Expedition
2-Year-Old 9-Year-Old
🐛 2yo — "Muddy Safari"

Fill a bin with cloud dough (8 cups flour + 1 cup baby oil) or dry oats. Add plastic bugs and small shovels for digging fun.

🧊 9yo — "Frozen Archaeologist"

Freeze plastic dinosaurs or camp tokens in a block of ice the night before. Give them a spray bottle of warm water, salt & a plastic tool to chip them free!

🌊 9yo — "Muddy Expedition" (alt or add-on)

Set up a second bin with cloud dough (8 cups flour + 1 cup baby oil) or mud. Hide small treasures, bones, or camp tokens inside. Give them tools to dig, sift & discover — full expedition mode!

10:20
AM
KiwiCo Crate Discovery
Both Kids
🌳 Outside on a blanket

Unpack the KiwiCrates in the fresh air. The perfect window for focused, independent building.

  • Your 9-year-old can follow the blueprint solo.
  • Give hands-on guidance to the toddler's crate.
11:00
AM
The Great Basecamp Hunt
9-Year-Old 2-Year-Old
📋 9yo — Scavenger Checklist

Give them a written checklist with riddles — "Find something smooth, something shaped like a Y, 5 pieces of kindling for tonight."

🎨 2yo — Color Wheel Hunt

Paint a color wheel on a paper plate with clothespins. They match a leaf to the green spot, a flower to the yellow spot — nature color-matching!

11:40
AM
Mess Hall Lunch
Both Kids
🧺 Camp-Style Eating
  • Sit on a picnic blanket — no tables allowed!
  • "Camp skewers" — fruit or cheese & meat cubes on sticks.
  • Hot dogs or sandwiches wrapped in foil.
12:20
PM
Lego Basecamp Builders
Both Kids
🏕️ The Challenge

Transition to calm, low-energy independent play. Challenge them to build a "Camp Site" out of their Lego sets!

🔑 Parent Hack: Perfect buffer zone if your 2-year-old needs an afternoon nap. If they sleep, the 9-year-old can dive deep into their Lego build completely uninterrupted.
1:00
PM
Splash Camp & Water Play
Both Kids
🫧 Beat the Afternoon Slump

Fill a shallow plastic storage bin with water, dish soap & sponges.

  • Wash their plastic camp toys.
  • Scrub the rocks they found on the hunt.
  • Target-practice with spray bottles against a fence.
3:00
PM
Reading & Worksheets
Both Kids
📖 Wind-Down Learning Time

Cozy up with books and worksheets — the perfect calm after all the outdoor adventure.

  • 9-year-old: independent reading or activity worksheets.
  • 2-year-old: coloring pages, tracing, or a picture book together.
4:00
PM
Slime Lab!
Both Kids
🌈 Camp Science Station

Time to get gloriously goopy! Set up a slime-making station outside for easy cleanup.

  • 9-year-old: mix their own batch with food coloring & glitter.
  • 2-year-old: squish, stretch & poke — pure sensory joy!

🪵 Late Afternoon — Pitch the Tent!

After the high-density day, transition into pitching the tent together.

The 9-year-old can help feed the tent poles through the canopy — a real job with real responsibility.

The 2-year-old's official title? Camp Inspector — testing sleeping pads and bags from the inside.

🔥 6:00 PM — Silva Family Campout Dinner

The main event! Gather the whole family around the campsite for a real campout dinner.

Think foil-wrapped meals, s'mores, hot dogs on sticks, and eating under the open sky.

Tonight the backyard becomes base camp — and the Silva family becomes a crew of true adventurers.
